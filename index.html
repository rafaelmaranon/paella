Paella Overview

The Story of Paella

Paella originated in the Valencia region of Spain in the 19th century. Farmers and laborers would cook rice with whatever ingredients they had available - typically rabbit, chicken, beans, and vegetables - in a large flat pan over an open fire.

The dish gets its name from the pan it's cooked in - "paella" means "pan" in Valencian. What started as a humble peasant dish has become one of Spain's most iconic culinary exports.

Paella in the United States

Today, paella has gained tremendous popularity in the United States, becoming a favorite at restaurants, food festivals, and home kitchens. The dish's communal nature and impressive presentation make it perfect for gatherings and celebrations.

In 2019, Galbis, a Spanish company, set the Guinness World Record for creating the largest paella ever made. This incredible feat took place in Valencia, Spain, and required:

  • Over 1,200 kg of rice
  • 2,400 kg of chicken
  • 1,200 kg of rabbit
  • 600 kg of green beans
  • 300 kg of tomatoes
  • A massive 20-meter diameter paella pan

This record-breaking paella could feed over 110,000 people and showcased the incredible scale and community spirit that paella represents.
